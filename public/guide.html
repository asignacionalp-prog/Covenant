Step 05Businesses · partners · clients

The three core entities that everything else hangs off.

Covenant's data model:

You (CEO)
  └─ Businesses (revenue streams you own)
       ├─ Clients (who pays your business)
       └─ Partners (people who do the work)

Businesses

Sidebar → Businesses → + Add business.

  • Name — e.g. "TrueVine Home Office"
  • Type — Service (you sell hours/skills) or Product (you sell goods)
  • Industry, Description, Start date, Logo

You can have multiple businesses. Income, partners, and obligations are tracked per business.

⬚ Screenshot 07 Add Business modal — form fields for name, type (radio: Service/Product), industry, description, start date, status, logo upload. Gold "Save" button at bottom.
guide-images/07-add-business.png

Partners

Sidebar → Partners → + Add partner.

Partner forms are long because they hold government IDs and pay configuration. Key fields:

  • Personal info: name, contact, DOB, address
  • Government IDs: SSS, TIN, PhilHealth, Pag-Ibig (optional but needed for statutory deductions)
  • Pay: monthly salary + currency, hours per day (typically 8), hourly rate (auto-computed or override)
  • Tithe: tick if enrolled in partner-tithing, set their tithe percent (typically 10)

Clients

Sidebar → Clients → + Add client.

  • Name, Business (which of yours serves them), Position, Type (Full-time / Part-time / Gig), Currency
  • Assigned partner (optional)
  • Start date, Status
  • ⚜ Migrated client checkbox — only tick if you started working with them BEFORE adopting Covenant AND already gave firstfruits on past payments. Skips auto-firstfruit on their first Covenant payment.

Step 06Daily workflow

Attendance, devotion, Sunday service, church activities.

Attendance

Sidebar → Attendance. Every active partner gets a row with the current month laid out as a calendar grid.

Click any day to cycle through statuses:

  • · empty — not logged
  • Present (green) — they showed up
  • Absent (red) — they didn't show up
  • Half-day (orange)
  • Leave (blue) — paid leave / approved absence
  • No work (gray) — weekend or holiday

For Present days, click into the partner row to set late minutes, undertime minutes, or overtime minutes.

Step 08Salaries & payroll

Two ways to pay partners. Pick the right one.

Option A — Manual salary entry

Sidebar → Salaries → + Record salary. Fields: partner, amount, currency, period, date, optional client allocation.

Manual entries don't apply attendance deductions. The amount you type is what's recorded. Use this for one-off payments — bonuses, advances, settlements, mid-month corrections.

Option B — Payroll runs (the proper way)

Sidebar → Payroll runs → + New run. Five steps:

  1. Pick frequency (monthly / semi-monthly / bi-weekly / weekly)
  2. Pick start date / end date / pay date
  3. Click Generate → the system pulls every active partner, computes gross pay (hourly rate × hours worked, minus late/UT/absent), statutory deductions (SSS / PhilHealth / Pag-Ibig), tithe withholding (if enrolled), and net pay.
  4. Review the lines. Add custom additions (bonus, holiday pay) or other deductions (loans, advances, penalties) per partner.
  5. Allocate each partner's salary across the clients they worked on. Click Post run when ready.
⬚ Screenshot 13 Payroll Run detail page — header showing frequency, period, paydate, status badge ("draft" amber). Below: a table of partner lines with columns for gross, attendance deduction, statutory deductions, tithe, net pay, and allocation buttons. Bottom: action bar with "Recompute draft", "To Review", "Post run" buttons.
guide-images/13-payroll-run.png
⚠Posted runs can't be edited. But you can reverse them: open the run → "Reverse this run" → creates a paired reversal (preserving audit trail). Then generate a new run with corrections.

Step 09Obligations

The core of Covenant — auto-computed from your payments and salaries.

Sidebar → Obligations. Four pools:

PoolWhat it isHow it's computed
FirstfruitsFirst income from each NEW client/business + January reset100% of net income for the firstfruit period
Tithes10% of net income (after firstfruit)tithe rate × net income per period per client
Church Project10% of net income (after firstfruit)CP rate × net income per period per client
Partner TithesTithe withheld from each partner's paypartner's tithe % × their net pay
What's "net income"? For each client, in each period: payments received – (gross salary – attendance deduction) × allocation %. Labor cost is post-attendance, not gross — when a partner is late or absent, that wage savings stays inside the company and doesn't reduce client revenue.

Recording invoice payments

Open the invoice → + Record payment → enter amount, currency, exchange rate, date. Multiple payments per invoice are allowed (partial payments). When the total paid matches the invoice total, status auto-flips to Paid.

FX variance

Scenario: You billed at exchange rate 61 but the client paid at rate 60. Here's the right move:
  1. Record the payment using the actual rate received (60)
  2. Invoice marks paid (USD amount matches)
  3. The ₱1,000 PHP difference is a realized FX loss
  4. Log it as an Expense entry with category "FX loss" or "Exchange rate variance", amount ₱1,000, dated same as payment
Your books then correctly show: income at booking rate ₱61,000, FX loss ₱1,000, net ₱60,000 hitting the bank. Audit trail preserved on both sides.

Step 13Settings & data export

Take your data with you — anytime, no questions asked.

Sidebar → Settings.

Your Data

Click ⬇ Export my data (JSON) any time to download a portable backup of your entire workspace. Save it to Google Drive / Dropbox / a USB key. Useful for year-end archive, sending to your accountant, or migrating off Covenant later. Your data is yours; we never lock you in.

Sign-in & security

Set or change your password here. Use at least 8 characters. After changing, your existing session keeps working — but next time you sign in, use the new password.

Migration & Bulk Import

If you have prior data in spreadsheets:

  • Time entries (attendance) — CSV import
  • Client payments — CSV import

Download the CSV templates first, fill them in, upload. Both are non-destructive — they add to existing data.

Step 14Troubleshooting

When something goes sideways.

"I can't sign in"

  1. Make sure you're typing the email you paid with
  2. Try "Already paid but never set up your account?" on the sign-in page — works if you stopped before finishing setup
  3. Forgot password? Email me directly during beta — I'll reset it for you. Self-service reset comes with v1.1 once email delivery is fixed.

"The sidebar shows ⚠ Sync failed"

Your changes haven't reached the cloud. Possible causes:

  • Internet dropped briefly → click the sidebar indicator to retry
  • Browser tab is rate-limited (rare) → reload (Ctrl+R)
  • Server-side issue → message me

Your local edits are not lost — they stay in the page until sync succeeds.

"The page is blank or stuck on loading"

  1. Hard-refresh: Ctrl+Shift+R (Cmd+Shift+R on Mac)
  2. Check browser console (F12 → Console) for red errors — paste them to me
  3. Try in incognito/private window — rules out cached state
  4. Check your internet connection

"I see other people's data"

⚠That should be impossible. Multi-tenancy is enforced server-side. If you genuinely think it's happening, stop and message me immediately.
